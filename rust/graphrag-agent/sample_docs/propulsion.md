# Propulsion at Port Halcyon

The Port Halcyon facility is the Aster Program's propulsion center.
Two engine families are developed there: the IPX-9 ion stack and the Hearthfire methalox engine.

The IPX-9 is a gridded ion thruster running on xenon propellant.
It powers the Helia Probe and is slated for the program's future outer-system missions.
The design emphasizes a quiet power bus so that sensitive plasma instruments can operate while the thruster fires (a requirement pushed through by Dr.
Sefa Lindqvist after early Corona Imaging Array tests showed interference).

Hearthfire is the opposite philosophy: a robust, throttleable methalox engine for the Talos Lander's powered descent.
Engineer Rohan Mehta leads the Hearthfire team and famously test-fires the engine every Friday, a tradition Port Halcyon staff call the weekend
whistle.

Both engine programs share a cryogenic test stand funded by Veridian Orbital.
Scheduling conflicts on that stand were a recurring sore point until flight director Ines Okonkwo brokered the alternating-week agreement in 2037.
