# The Aster Program

The Aster Program is a fictional deep-space exploration initiative run by the Meridian Space Agency.
Founded in 2031 under director Dr. Mara Voss, the program coordinates three flagship missions: the Helia Probe, the Kestrel Station, and the Talos
Lander.

The program's stated goal is to characterize the heliosphere boundary and to demonstrate closed-loop life support beyond lunar orbit.
Funding comes jointly from the Meridian Space Agency and the private consortium Veridian Orbital, which supplies launch services through its Skylark
heavy-lift vehicle.

Program management is split between two centers.
Mission design and navigation are handled at the Calloway Flight Center, while spacecraft assembly and propulsion testing happen at the Port Halcyon
facility on the coast.
A long-standing rivalry between the two sites was largely resolved after the Unified Operations Charter of 2034, championed by Dr. Mara Voss and
flight director Ines Okonkwo.
