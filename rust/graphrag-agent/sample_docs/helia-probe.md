# The Helia Probe

The Helia Probe is the first flagship mission of the Aster Program.
Launched in 2036 on a Skylark heavy-lift vehicle, the probe studies the solar corona and the inner heliosphere from a highly elliptical orbit.

Helia carries four instruments.
The primary instrument, the Corona Imaging Array, was built at the Calloway Flight Center under principal investigator
Dr. Sefa Lindqvist. The probe also carries a magnetometer boom, a plasma wave sensor, and the Dustfall particle counter contributed by Veridian
Orbital.

The mission's most publicized moment came in 2038, when the Corona Imaging Array captured the first resolved images of a switchback cascade in the
solar wind.
Dr. Sefa Lindqvist and Dr. Mara Voss presented the findings at the Meridian Science Assembly, crediting the probe's unusually quiet power bus (a
design carried over from the Talos Lander program by engineer Rohan Mehta.)

Helia is powered by the Aster Program's standard ion propulsion stack, the IPX-9, developed at Port Halcyon. The IPX-9 uses xenon propellant and a
gridded ion thruster design tuned for long-duration low-thrust cruises.
