# Kestrel Station

Kestrel Station is the Aster Program's crewed outpost, stationed at the Earth-Sun L2 point since 2039.
The station demonstrates closed-loop life support for crews of six on rotations of up to four hundred days.

The station's life support system, named Greenloop, recycles water and air through a hybrid of physical filtration and an algae bioreactor.
Greenloop was designed by Dr. Amara Sole's team at Port Halcyon, and its bioreactor cartridges fly to the station aboard Skylark resupply flights
twice a year.

Kestrel Station also hosts the Farsight telescope, which piggybacks on the station's precision pointing platform. Farsight time is shared between the
Meridian Space Agency and university partners through a proposal process chaired by Dr. Sefa Lindqvist.

Flight director Ines Okonkwo commanded the station's first expedition.
Her crew included engineer Rohan Mehta, who retrofitted the station's docking adapter in a celebrated nine-hour spacewalk after a Skylark cargo
capsule arrived with a bent berthing ring.
