# The Talos Lander

The Talos Lander is the Aster Program's planetary surface mission, targeting the icy moon Erebos-2.
The lander is scheduled to launch in 2042 on a Skylark heavy-lift vehicle and will attempt the first powered descent onto a body with active
cryovolcanism.

Talos descends on a throttleable methalox engine called Hearthfire, built at Port Halcyon by the propulsion group under engineer Rohan Mehta.
Hearthfire is deliberately conservative: a single chamber, pintle injection, and deep throttle capability proven over two hundred test firings.

Once on the surface, Talos deploys the Burrow drill, a percussive ice drill designed to reach liquid brine pockets two meters down.
Samples feed the onboard Wetlab, a miniature chemistry suite whose detection assays were designed by Dr. Amara Sole.

Navigation during descent uses terrain-relative matching against maps produced by the Farsight telescope on Kestrel Station, an arrangement negotiated
by Dr. Mara Voss to keep the program's missions interdependent.
